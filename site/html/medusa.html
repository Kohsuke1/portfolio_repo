On an alien world where survival of the fitest is rule. The jellyfish-creature must eat to evolve and become the predator.

Medusa, the animation short, starts on an alien world where everything looks to be made out of flesh including planet matter. We see a little jellyfish-like organism peer out of one of the trees looking around for food. However, it isn't the only organism look for something to eat.

A pterodactyl-like creature emerges from the forest backdrop, spots the little jellyfish like creature, and lets out a blood-curdling cry to signal the hunt. Startled by the cry, the jellyfish-like organism makes a run for it, and the chase begins

It loses the pterodactyl-like creature by hinding behind a tree. Scared and hungry, it looks around for the predator and for some food, and luckily a smaller worm-like organism crosses the it's path.

The jellyfish-like creature stretches it's tentacle out to snatch the worm-like organism and stuffs it into it's mouth. It then evolves into a larger and more capable organism. It feasts some more to continue it's transformation and eventually runs into the pterodactyl-like creature again.

The year long process

First, we illustrated and drew each frame with pencil so that mistakes can be easily corrected. After completing the pencil illustrations, we needed to stencil it over with a sharper or heavy marker so that the scanner can pick it up clearly as it made a digital copy. Next, we colored each illustrations in photoshop pixel by pixel, and then animated each scene of the characters with DigiCel Flipbook. Lastly, compiled in Adobe After Effects for the background and sound.
